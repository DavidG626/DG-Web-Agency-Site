What This Score Measures

The Same Tools Google Uses to Evaluate Your Site

This score runs your website through Google Lighthouse, the same engine Google uses to measure site quality. It scores four categories that directly affect whether customers find you, trust you, and contact you.

Performance

How fast your site loads on a mobile phone. Google has confirmed that page speed is a separate ranking factor, which means even if your SEO score is perfect, a slow site will still show up lower in search results. Slow pages also lose visitors before they ever see what you offer. Every second of load time costs you clicks, calls, and customers.

Accessibility

Whether your site works for people with disabilities, including those using screen readers, keyboard navigation, or assistive devices. Poor accessibility limits your audience and can create legal risk under ADA guidelines.

Best Practices

Technical health checks including HTTPS, secure connections, modern code standards, and correct image formats. Issues here can trigger browser warnings that make your site look unsafe to visitors.

SEO

Whether your site follows the technical requirements Google needs to properly crawl, index, and rank your pages. Missing titles, broken meta tags, and poor structure all hurt your visibility in search results.
